Visualization of World Happiness

This Visualization aims to show which countries/regions are to most happy. It also shows if there are other factors that corrolate with Happiness

Happiness Score Choropleth Map Overview

The choropleth map provides a visual representation of the happiness scores for each country. Each country is shaded based on its happiness score, with darker shades indicating higher scores and lighter shades representing lower scores. This allows for a quick and intuitive comparison of happiness levels across countries, offering insights into the distribution and variations in global happiness.

Trust and World Happiness: Bar Chart Overview

The bar chart visually represents the correlation between trust levels and world happiness. Each bar on the chart corresponds to a specific region or country, and the height of the bar indicates the level of trust within that region. The x-axis displays the different regions or countries, while the y-axis represents the trust levels, ranging from low to high. Regions with higher bars depict a stronger correlation between trust and happiness, emphasizing the importance of trust in fostering a happier society.

GDP and Happiness Score Scatter Plot Overview

The scatter plot visually represents the correlation between GDP (Gross Domestic Product) and happiness scores for each region. Each point on the plot represents a country, with its position indicating its GDP and happiness score. A dropdown menu allows the user to select a specific region, highlighting the countries in that region on the scatter plot. This interactive feature enables users to focus on a particular region and observe the relationship between GDP and happiness scores within that region.
